Warming-up

We warm up the body from top to bottom. Starting from the neck, shoulders, arms (elbows, wrists), back, hips, legs (knees, ankles)

1st Exercise

It's not important how you stand up, but how you squat, that is, squat as slowly as possible so that there is a negative load on the hip and this works for strength and endurance. If you have severe knee pain, stop here. You will do 4 sets with as many repetitions as possible according to your own feeling, no more than 10.

2nd Exercise

Sitting on a chair with your back straight and your knee slightly raised, extend and hold the leg in question forward, doing it slowly and without touching the foot to the ground during the exercise 4x10 on each leg

3th Exercise

The important thing here is to keep your supporting leg slightly bent and your back straight, go down as far as possible without bending your knee more than indicated, and stand up 4x10 on each side.

4st Exercise

The important thing here is that the support is on the heels and the feet are shoulder-width apart 3x12

5st Exercise

Lean forward towards the wall until you feel a slight stretch in your calf, then begin to lift yourself up on your toes as high as possible 4x10-15
